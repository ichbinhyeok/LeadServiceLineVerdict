# 01 Query And User Map

## 1) Core jobs to be done
- Tell me whether my water service line is known, potential, or not lead.
- Tell me what the utility inventory says and how to verify my address.
- Tell me who pays for replacement and what the real cost range is.
- Tell me whether I should replace, filter, test, or wait for a program.
- Tell me what to do if I am buying or selling the home now.

## 2) Priority user segments
### Highest-intent users
- Homeowners who received a known or potential lead service line notice.
- Buyers who need negotiation leverage before closing.
- Sellers who need disclosure and repair guidance.
- Residents comparing utility inventory status against an address-level notice.

### Secondary users
- Landlords and property managers handling tenant concerns.
- Realtors and attorneys who need a clean local explainer.
- Contractors and filter providers who want a trusted referral page.

## 3) Query families by funnel stage
### "Need to know if this applies"
- `{city} lead service line`
- `{utility} lead service line inventory`
- `{utility} known or potential lead line`
- `what does potential lead service line mean`
- `how do i check my water service line`

### "Need to act now"
- `{utility} lead service line notification`
- `{city} lead pipe notice`
- `replace lead service line program {state}`
- `lead service line lookup {utility}`
- `what to do after lead line letter`

### "Need a budget"
- `{city} lead service line replacement cost`
- `who pays for lead service line replacement`
- `lead service line replacement cost estimator`
- `private side lead pipe replacement cost`

### "Need a workaround or interim step"
- `water filter for lead service line`
- `lead water test kit`
- `does replacing the service line remove lead from water`
- `how long to use a filter after lead notice`

### "Need to decide in a transaction"
- `lead service line disclosure buyer`
- `seller credit lead service line`
- `should i ask for a lead line replacement before closing`
- `lead pipe inspection during home purchase`

## 4) Canonical SEO unit selection
- Default canonical entity: utility or water authority.
- City pages exist when the city is part of the user query, but the utility remains the governing source.
- Program pages should exist for state or utility clusters where the replacement rules or subsidies are materially different.

## 6) User state model
### State A: Unaware
- User has not received a notice.
- Goal: expose the inventory and make the issue concrete.

### State B: Notified
- User received a known or potential lead line letter.
- Goal: explain the meaning, urgency, and exact next steps.

### State C: Budgeting
- User wants cost, financing, or program help.
- Goal: show replacement range, subsidy options, and who to call.

### State D: Transaction
- User is buying or selling now.
- Goal: present negotiation language, disclosure, and timing.

### State E: Interim protection
- User cannot replace immediately.
- Goal: guide testing and filter selection without overstating the protection.

## 7) AEO targets
- "What does a potential lead service line mean?"
- "Who pays for lead service line replacement in {city}?"
- "How much does lead service line replacement cost in {city}?"
- "Should I use a filter after a lead service line notice?"
- "What should a buyer ask for if the house has a lead service line?"

## 8) What to avoid
- Generic plumbing explainers with no inventory or utility mapping.
- Pages that collapse service line, interior plumbing, and fixture lead into one bucket.
- National cost articles that never answer who pays locally.
- Pages that imply a filter is the same as replacement.
