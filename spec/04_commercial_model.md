# 04 Commercial Model

## 1) Revenue thesis
LeadServiceLineVerdict should monetize as a utility-intent local sponsor business with affiliate support, not as a broad national content site.

## 2) Primary monetization paths
- Water filter affiliates
- Water test kit or certified lab affiliates
- Local plumber or line replacement sponsor placements
- Utility or metro program referral placements where allowed
- Buyer/seller lead capture for replacement consults

## 3) Secondary monetization paths
- Premium local provider profiles
- Program navigator pages for cities with large inventories
- Referral pages for home inspectors, real estate agents, and property managers

## 4) Offer design
### Starter sponsor
- One metro or utility cluster
- featured CTA on replacement-cost and filter pages
- one location or geography

### Premium sponsor
- featured profile
- call tracking
- multiple utility or city pages
- presence across notification, cost, and buyer/seller pages

## 5) CTA logic
- Inventory page -> official source first, sponsor second
- Notification page -> action CTA above the fold
- Replacement cost page -> sponsor CTA and request a quote
- Filter page -> product CTA and testing CTA
- Buyer/seller page -> consult CTA and checklist CTA

## 6) Lead handling rules
- No multi-buyer resale without explicit consent.
- Keep forms short: name, phone, city, utility, issue type, homeownership status.
- Track source page, geography, and intent for every lead.
- If a sponsor is featured, disclose it clearly.

## 7) Early sales motion
- Start with direct outreach to local plumbers, line replacement contractors, and testing or filter providers.
- Sell on intent, not traffic volume.
- Use exact phrases like "known or potential lead line notice" and "replacement decision support."
- Offer geography-specific placement, not generic banner inventory.
